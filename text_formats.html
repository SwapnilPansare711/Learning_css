Once in a village, a farmer lived with his family. He had three sons. The farmer was very poor and had very little land for farming. He was hardworking, but even then, he could not afford good clothes for his sons. At that time, the king did not have a prince. Because of this, the king was worried and decided to adopt a boy from his relatives. He invited all his relatives along with their children for an interview. One day, the king’s men came to invite the poor farmer. After receiving the invitation, the farmer became scared. He thought, “What mistake have I made?” But he had no other option, so he went to the palace with his three sons. His eldest son was 12 years old, the middle one was 8 years old, and the youngest was 6 years old. The next day, the king’s ministers sat like a panel to select the prince. They called each child one by one. They asked many questions, and at the end, they asked a common question to every candidate: “Why have you come here?” By midday, the farmer’s eldest son entered for the interview. After answering a few questions, he was asked the final question. He replied, “I have come here because my father told me to come.” After him, the youngest son went in. He stayed silent during the questions, and when asked the final question, he became shy and ran back to his father. By evening, they were all disappointed. At the end, only one candidate was left—the 8-year-old son. When he entered the room, the queen directly asked him, “Why have you come here?” Without taking any time, the boy answered with a confident smile, “I have come here to become a king.” The panel was impressed, and the boy was selected as the prince. And today, we know this boy as King Sayajirao Gaikwad of Baroda.
