The next day, the king’s ministers sat like a panel to select the prince. They called each child one by one. They asked many questions, and at the end, they asked a common question to every candidate: “Why have you come here?” By midday, the farmer’s eldest son entered for the interview. After answering a few questions, he was asked the final question. He replied, “I have come here because my father told me to come.” After him, the youngest son went in. He stayed silent during the questions, and when asked the final question, he became shy and ran back to his father. By evening, they were all disappointed. At the end, only one candidate was left—the 8-year-old son. When he entered the room, the queen directly asked him, “Why have you come here?” Without taking any time, the boy answered with a confident smile, “I have come here to become a king.” The panel was impressed, and the boy was selected as the prince. And today, we know this boy as King Sayajirao Gaikwad of Baroda
